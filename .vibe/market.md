# Market

## Competitors

| Competitor | What They Do Well | Where They Fall Short | Our Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| SEC/DOJ Online Portals | Official government intake. | Poor UX; cloud database storage risks early exposure; no timeline helper tools. | Offline-first evidence collection and local timeline drafting. |
| General Encryption Tools (VeraCrypt) | Industrial-grade file system encryption. | Complex setup; no legal report generation or contractor vetting. | Specialized False Claims Act workflow (USAspending, Typst reports). |
| Standard Notes Apps (Notion/Evernote) | Beautiful editing and timeline templates. | Cloud syncing leaks metadata; no local-only offline enforcement. | Zero-cloud architecture and automatic EXIF metadata stripping. |

## Positioning
Open Season is the only offline-first, metadata-scrubbed workspace built specifically for whistleblowers compiling evidence under the False Claims Act.

## Target Market
Whistleblowers and Pro Se advocates investigating government contractor fraud within the United States (specifically targets of the False Claims Act, 31 U.S.C. §§ 3729–3733).

## Opportunities
- **Increase in government spending**: Higher volumes of federal contracts increase the potential for fraud and the demand for Qui Tam filing assistance.
- **Privacy-conscious trends**: Growing skepticism of cloud providers and centralized storage increases the value of local-first tools.

## Threats
- **Operating System restrictions**: App store policies or sandboxing updates in Windows/Mac could limit local file system write scopes.
- **Complexity of legal templates**: If False Claims Act standards change rapidly, templates must be manually updated to prevent compiling invalid PDFs.
