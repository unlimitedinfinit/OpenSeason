# Users

## Personas

### The Concerned Employee (Whistleblower)
- **Who**: An accountant, contract administrator, or engineer at a government contractor who notices double-billing, shoddy work, or fake invoices.
- **Goal**: Safely document the fraud, estimate the contract values, and draft a high-quality disclosure statement to hand over to a lawyer or the DOJ.
- **Frustration**: Keeping evidence files on a work PC or personal cloud is highly dangerous. Standard document editors leave metadata traces that can reveal their identity.
- **Fear**: Retaliation, losing their job, being blacklisted from the industry, or having their evidence leaked/ruined.
- **Technical Comfort**: Moderate. Uses spreadsheets and databases but is not an IT security expert.

### The Pro Se Advocate
- **Who**: An activist or independent researcher tracking local or state-level corruption.
- **Goal**: Draft a clear timeline of fraud events and verify targets against public contracts over $100k.
- **Frustration**: Finding clean data on government spending is difficult, and compiling multiple sources into a coherent court document is exhausting.
- **Fear**: Missing a filing deadline, formatting their document poorly, or having their files intercepted by the target contractor.
- **Technical Comfort**: High. Enthusiastic about local-first tools and cryptographic security.

## Emotional Reality
- **Users are highly anxious**: Whistleblowing is a stressful, high-stakes process. Users fear for their livelihoods and safety. They need immediate confirmation that their evidence is encrypted, metadata-scrubbed, and saved locally.
- **Users need absolute privacy**: They do not trust any external cloud syncing or servers. They choose Open Season because it runs completely offline.

## Common Objections
- **How do I know this is really offline?**: Users want to make sure the app isn't silently sending telemetry or case documents to the cloud.
- **What happens if I lose my password?**: Users get worried about losing access to their files and need to understand the zero-trust recovery trade-off.

- "The fact that this app strips EXIF data from my photos of contractor shortcuts is a lifesaver. I didn't realize how easily those could trace back to me."
